The Stories of my Pets

This webpage shares with the readers the different pets that I have had. Growing up, I have had dogs, small turtles, and a chicken. As an adult, I have had my own dog and two cats. Here is a bit about my pets.

  • Cleo
  • Chico
  • Lucky and Buddy
  • Proudy
  • Oscar and Ralph
  • Daisy
  • Patches
  • Chance

Cleo

As a kid, we got a family dog, a dachshund mix, as a puppy. Her name was Cleo. I do not remember Cleo as a puppy, but her mother was my cousin's dog Cindy.

Chico

The second dog that we had was Chico. Chico and his brother were dumped out of a car in the neighborhood as young dogs. Our neighbor across the street saw the dogs being dumped and took them in. He and his wife kept the white dog and offered my mom the black dog. When I came home from school one day, there was a furry, black dog in the house! For quite awhile, the neighbors called their dog Boy, so we decided to name ours the Spanish equivalent, Chico. (They eventually gave Boy the name of Bandit instead.) Chico was a small, cool mutt. He wagged his tail up-and-down instead of side-to-side. He was a great dog to have through childhood.

Lucky and Buddy

During grade school, I also had two small turtles. I am not sure what kind they were, but I think they were baby red slider turtles. This kind of turtle used to be sold in pet stores, but I think they have since been banned...I believe because they carry salmonella. I named my turtles Lucky and Buddy after some dogs that I read about in a book. Sadly, Lucky and Buddy met a very unfortunate death and were buried in matchbox car boxes in the woods across the street. We conducted a small funeral attended by only myself, my brother, and my two sisters.

I did not have a photo available of Lucky and Buddy, so this is a stock photo from the internet.

Proudy

For Easter one year, my two sisters and I got baby chicks. They were Rhode Island Red chickens. My chick walked around with its head held up high, so I named him Proudy. Chico the dog liked following the chicks around with his nose to the ground, following their trail. We kept them in a special set-up in the basement (they got out to wander in the backyard and around the house under supervision). As they got bigger, my mom took them to a local feed store or somewhere like that to get them a more appropriate home.

I did not have a photo available of Proudy, so this is a stock photo from the internet.

Oscar and Ralph

During my grade school years, we also had two gerbils. They were named Oscar and Ralph. They enjoyed running in their HabiTrail wheel at night, shredding toilet paper rolls for bedding, and an occasional supervised exploration around the house. As he did with the chicks, Chico also loved to track the gerbils when we would let them loose.

I did not have a photo available of Oscar and Ralph, so this is a stock photo from the internet.

Daisy

As an adult, I always wanted a dog, so a few months after I bought my house, I adopted a brittany mix puppy and named her Daisy. Daisy was a great dog and a perfect companion. I won't say much more about Daisy, or I would just go on and on about how wonderful she was. I will just say that everyone that she met loved her and she once had an award-winning website of her own.

Patches

For many years, I fed three feral cats that hung out in the neighborhood. One day, a sickly calico cat showed up to eat with them. You could tell this calico had once had a home because she was very friendly to people. She hung around for several weeks before I decided that she would not be able to make it through winter outside. So, I brought her in and adopted her and doctored her back to health. I named her Patches. I got lucky with Patches - she was more dog-like than cat-like... maybe it was because she was so sick, or maybe her previous owners trained her, but she does not jump up on tables and kitchen counters and such.

Chance

The most recent pet added to my family is Chance. I adopted him last year from Friends of Parkville Animal Shelter (FOPAS) in order to keep Patches company and more active. I wasn't sure that the kitty was the right match for our little family of Patches and me - when I took him home from the adoption, I felt like I was taking a chance. So, I eventually decided to name him Chance. He is a buff-colored American Shorthair. In his nature, Chance is a true cat and he has taught Patches some unbecoming cat behavior. Despite his antics, Chance has become a beloved member of the family.

That is the summary of my life with pets thus far. I have also fostered several dogs for FOPAS over the years. Doing so has introduced me to the charms of some breeds that wouldn't normally be at the top of my list. But each dog won me over and found good homes for themselves. Now, with Patches and Chance, it will be awhile before I add another permanent or foster pet to the family.
